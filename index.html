Keep the device well away from the crib and at a low volume — soft enough that you could comfortably hold a quiet conversation nearby.

Why these settings

Color. Broadband white noise is the only spectrum with an infant trial behind it: 80% of newborns fell asleep within five minutes with white noise versus 25% without (Spencer et al., Arch Dis Child). Neonates respond most strongly to broad-spectrum sound (fMEG study). Pink noise's "deeper sleep" evidence is adult-only, and brown noise is not well studied in infants — so the recommended band is White–Pink, and Brown is offered but flagged as less studied.

Volume. The AAP found some infant sound machines exceed 85 dB — the level at which workplace hearing protection is required — and the effects of loud, long-duration exposure on the developing brain are unknown. This app cannot measure real loudness, so keep the volume quiet enough to talk over and place the device well away from the crib.

Consistency. The evidence favours consistency over any particular color, so your settings are saved and restored automatically each session.
